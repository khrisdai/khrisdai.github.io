Project MBTI

With a curated set of personas based on individuals' healthcare needs, preferences, and behaviors, project MBTI aims to simplify the decision-making process to empower you to make informed choices for your health coverage. Join the waitlist to learn more.

Elva, 26-year-old freelance writer

Elva is a 26-year-old freelance writer who reached the age cutoff and can no longer receive coverage through her parents.

Having never purchased health insurance for herself, she is unfamiliar with health insurance terminology like coinsurance, deductible, and out-of-pocket maximum. She also wants to know what financial assistance she can receive, if any, to help her pay for coverage.

Rami, 30-year-old software engineer

Rami is a 30-year-old software engineer who lost employer-provided coverage due to recent waves of tech layoffs. He needs basic coverage until he finds new employment that includes health insurance.

Rami needs help understanding the different choices: COBRA vs Marketplace vs short term plan.

Naomi, 37-year-old single mother

Naomi is a 37-year-old single mother with two children who recently changed jobs and her new employer does not offer health insurance coverage.

Obtaining cost effective coverage is a priority for her and her children. She needs help identify a plan tailored to her family's unique circumstances, including accessing Advance Payments of the Premium Tax Credits (APTC) to reduce her monthly premium by 50%.

Satoshi, 45-year-old in dual income family

Satoshi is a 45-year-old corporate manager who wants to figure out the best health insurance option for himself and his wife, both of whom have coverage through their respective employers.

He needs assistance in comparing the benefits, costs, and coverage limits of each plan and may seek guidance on coordinating benefits between their two policies, optimizing coverage for their family's medical expenses, and understanding potential out-of-pocket expenses.

Lynda, owner of a small business

Lynda is the owner of a small businesswho wants to offer coverage to her new employees. She recently learned of a new coverage option called an Individual Coverage Health Reimbursement Arrangement (ICHRA).

ICHRA gives her the option to offer her employees a monthly allowance of tax-free money to buy individual market health insurance that fits their unique needs. She wonders if this is the right approach.

Elias, 63-year-old early retiree

Elias is a 63-year-old early retiree who is not yet Medicare eligible but in need of health insurance.

Elias suffers from a host of chronic conditions, including diabetes, and needs a plan that includes his endocrinologist and local hospital in-network, does not have a high-deductible, and has a premium less than $500 per month.

Elias is not technologically savvy enough to research everything he needs online.
