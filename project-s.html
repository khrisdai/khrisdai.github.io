Project S@ndwich

We recently conducted a survey targeting first-generation American immigrants, aiming to gain insight into their parents' healthcare needs and identify any gaps. The results were surprising, so we have decided to share these findings with everyone.

Survey Population

Over 75% of the people surveyed say their parents stay in the U.S. for over 3 months. Most are visiting on a B2 visitor visa.

The age of their parents range from 55-80, with an average age of 65.

85% of them have one or more chronic conditions such as hypertension, diabetes.

2/3 of them take prescription drugs.

Length of Stay
Survey Results

60% purchased travel insurance or short-term health insurance.

The rest 40% say they did not purchase insurance because (1) they didn't find existing products valuable (e.g., too expensive and too restrictive) (2) didn't think it would be needed (3) not sure what to buy.

We ask survey participants how concerned they are about their ability to take care of their parents financially and emotionally. Most rated emotional concern to be higher than financial.

最近，我们进行了一项针对第一代美国移民的问卷调查，旨在深入了解他们父母的医疗需求以及目前存在的不足之处。调查结果出乎我们的意料，因此我们决定与大家分享这些发现。

调查对象

超过75%的受访者表示，他们的父母在美国逗留超过3个月。大多数持B2探亲签证。

父母的年龄在55至80岁之间，平均年龄为65岁。

85%患有一种或多种慢性病，如高血压、糖尿病等。

2/3的人需要服用处方药。

逗留时间
调查结果

60%购买了旅行保险或短期健康保险

剩下的40%表示他们没有购买保险，原因主要有三：（1）他们认为现有的产品价值不高（例如，价格太贵、限制太多）；（2）觉得不会用到；（3）不确定应该购买什么。

我们问调查参与者对于自己是否能够在经济层面和情感层面照顾好父母的担忧程度。大多数人认为情感上的担忧要大于经济上的担忧。
